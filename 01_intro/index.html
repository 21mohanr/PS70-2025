I looked through lots of old projects and thought about what could be both manageable and interesting. <p class = "margin"></p> <h4>Idea 1 : Subwoofer with bass control</h4> <p class = "margin"></p> I like electronic music but have sometimes found the bass on speakers that are commercially sold to be subpar. As such, I could build my own subwoofer that controls the bass level. On the base level, the project would include some cutting and designing of the enclosure (a box of sorts), but it could also have touch sensors or microphone sensors for increased user interaction. I am a bit struggling to understand how microcontroller programming fits into this project, but that might be because I don't actually know what microcontroller programming consists of! I have to fumble around for where my phone actually is, press snooze, and repeat the process when I want to snooze again. Instead, the idea would be to create an alarm clock that responds to motions such as waving or other disturbances. But why not have a lamp that becomes brighter in dimmer areas and shines less in brighter areas, thus saving electricity and manual work? The project would involve the creation of the physical structure of the lamp but also could use light sensors and such. How fancy the project could go (what type of light, user interaction features, etc.) may depend more on time.
